Email Communication

In my opinion, emails are still one of the most important ways to communicate, especially for formal situations. While texting apps are faster, I think emails are better for professional communication because they are more organised.

From my point of view, one big advantage of emails is that they are professional. They are commonly used in schools, workplaces, and other formal settings to send important messages. Emails have features like subject lines, signatures, and attachments, which make them great for sharing detailed information or documents. Emails began to be used in the 1970s, but they were improved in 1971. One year later, in 1972, Ray Tomlinson sent the first email, marking an important step in the development of digital communication. It was a simple message that said "QWERTYUIOP," but it marked the beginning of a communication revolution.

Another reason why emails are useful, is that they are accessible from anywhere in the world with an internet connection. Today, there are over 4.3 billion people using email globally, and this number is expected to grow to 4.6 billion by 2025. Additionally, about 6.5 billion people check their email every day, making it one of the most used communication tools worldwide. Every day, more than 306 billion emails are sent across the globe, proving their importance in daily life for school, work, and personal use.

However, I think emails have some problems too. For example, spam emails can be annoying and waste of time. Spam emails make up approximately 45% of all emails sent, which means nearly half of our inboxes can get filled with unnecessary messages! Moreover, emails can be slow compared to instant messaging apps, which are better for quick, casual conversations or group chats. Studies show that 54% of people prefer using WhatsApp for work-related communication. Additionally, a survey found that 72% of teenagers prefer messaging apps like Snapchat and Instagram for real-time interactions.

In conclusion, I believe emails are a very important tool for communication. Their tone, organizational features, and global reach make them essential for formal situations. Using emails for official matters and chat apps for casual conversations is the best way to communicate effectively. Emails may have their challenges, but they remain an important part of modern life.
