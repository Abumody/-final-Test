Teen Mobile Phone Usage

Allowing teens to have mobile phone is one of the recent provoking debates around the world. Despite the good impact of mobile phone in people's life, mobile addiction is a real problem and a cause of great concern especially for teenagers.

There is no doubt that smartphone can be beneficial for teens as they use it to connect with peers, seek help on school assignments and help them get organized. According to the survey conducted in the United States, 31% of the teen students use their smart phones to do their homework and assignments. Since teens face intense levels of pressure, mobile phone use can help them relieve stress through spending time expressing their ideas and thoughts in various topics of their interest.

On the other hand, excessive use of mobiles causes mental health issues. For example, anxiety can result due to the time wasted on the phone. This causes stress because the teens now lack time to complete their schoolwork. Another common bad effect of smartphone use that it interferes teens normal sleep routine. It causes Insomnia or sleep disturbances related to frequent checking. This leads to major changes in their mood and behaviour. Furthermore, recent studies revealed that spending too much time using mobile phones by teens affects their relationships with their parents and causes a lack in communication, which leads to conflicts.

According to recent studies, teenagers have the highest rate of mobile phone addiction. They spend an average of nine hours a day on their smartphones, which means two hours more than the normal sleeping hours. Moreover, 50% of teenagers feel that they are addicted to their phones. Pew Research Report in 2018 showed that 45% of teens use the Internet "almost constantly." Based on this report, 50% of teenage girls are "near-constant" online users, compared to 39% of teenage boys. Overall, 66% of teenagers feel bad-tempered when they do not have their smartphones.

Another study, titled "The New Normal: Parents, Teens and Mobile Devices in Mexico," was conducted by the University of Southern California found that in Mexico, 45% of teens said they spend too much time on their mobile devices, compared with 39% in the United States, 32% in the United Kingdom and 17% in Japan.

Finally, it is important to educate teens on how to use smartphones wisely to create and maintain a healthy balance. Parents can help limit their teen's cell phone use in several ways. Parents can set rules and remind their teens that having a phone is a privilege, not a right.
