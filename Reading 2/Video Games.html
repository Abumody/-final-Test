Video Games: Benefits and Effects

For years, video games have been criticized for causing problems for gamers. However, recent studies have proved that video games can improve a human's body and mind.

To start with, Video games can develop physical skills. For example, based on a study, pre-school children who play interactive games can show improvement in motor skills. They can kick, catch, and throw a ball better than others. Also, video games are useful for surgeons who do microsurgery. A study conducted in Boston found that surgeons who played video games were 27% faster and made 37% fewer errors than those who did not. Their vision has improved also, particularly in telling the difference between shades of grey, which helped them in reading X-rays.

In addition, video games are useful for some patients who suffer from physical problems due to mental stroke. An experiment showed that some patients could control their hands and wrists again after playing video games for one month.

Games also benefit a variety of brain functions, including decision-making. People who play action games make decisions 25% faster than those who do not play this type of game. It was also found that the best gamers could make choices and act on them up to six times a second.

Gaming can also help reduce psychological problems. A research team at Oxford University found that playing the video game puzzle called "Tetris", shortly after facing upsetting situations can prevent people from keeping troubling memories.

Some studies claimed that playing violent video games can increase aggression levels in gamers. However, a study conducted in 2014 proved the opposite. The Researchers found that playing "death-bloated" could cause a decrease of real-life violence because it let gamers show and release their aggression through the game.

In conclusion, I believe that all video games have positive and negative effects. However, its negative impact can be reduced. Experts say that adults should have a limit screen time of eight hours for work plus two hours for entertainment. They suggest using a timer, leaving the device somewhere away or turning off device notifications. What works best with me is using 20-20-20 rule which means every 20 minutes, I look at something 20 feet way for 20 seconds.
